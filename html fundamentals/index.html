Choosing Which Workout Routine Works For Me

Posted by Sherif Atitebi on Tuesday, January 22nd 2051

Picking a workout routine to follow can be a difficult task with all the different information out there about workout styles. I went through thousands of minutes of youtube videos and articles to find which one will work for me.

I did a trial and error for all types of workout routine there is and I found myself to like the Push-Pull-Leg routine. What is it? I will explain in this post.

What is the Push-Pull-Leg Routine

The Push-Pull-Leg routine aka PPL is one of the popular routines for gym heads in the fitness world. It is basically working your Pushing muscles, pulling muscles, and your leg muscles on different days. You focus on each muscle group per gym per gym session. There are other types of gym routines out there which you can follow.

The pushing muscles are primarily your chest, triceps, and shoulders. They are engaged when you push objects. Chest press, military press, tricep extension and so on work the push groups. The pulling muscles are your biceps, back, traps, and lats.There really is no end to the exercises you can do to work these muscle group. And finally your leg muscles, primarily the quads, hamstring, calves, hips, adductors, and the myriad of other muscles your leg uses. They can be worked on by doing squats, leg press, calf raises (seated and standing), lunges.

Why do I need three different days to work these muscles? Well you don't, It is your prefrence how you want to build your workout routine. Like I said earlier in the post there are other routines that are widely known, and you can choose from these also. They are;

  1. Push-Pull-Leg
  2. Gym Bros routine
  3. Full body workout
  4. Upper Lower
  5. Personalised Workout

Why PPL?

PPL is for those who have the time to go everyday. If your job or schedule doesn't permit you going 4-5 days a week the PPL isn't for you. The full body workout routine is more on your scale if you can only go 2-3 times.

Here is what one of my 3 day PPL routine looks like (set and reps not included)

PUSH

  • Barbell Press
  • Alternated dumbell Press
  • Chwst flies w/ triceps extension
  • Lateral raises
  • Dips

PULL

  • Pull Ups
  • Rack Pulls
  • Hammer Curls
  • Reverse Grip row
  • Cable Curls
  • Cable Row
  • Dumbell Curls

LEGS

  • Squats
  • Leg Press superset w/ Calves raises
  • Hip Adductors
  • Standing Calf Raises
  • Quad Extensions
